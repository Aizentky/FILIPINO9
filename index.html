Music

Elements of Music

The four main elements that make up music production and performance.

Element 1

Instrumentation

Instrumentation refers to the selection and combination of instruments used in a musical piece. It gives the timbre or color of the sound.

Types of Instruments

String (guitar, violin)
Wind (flute, trumpet)
Percussion (drums, piano)
Electronic (synthesizer)

💡 Example:

A rock band uses electric guitar, bass guitar, drums, and vocals — this is the instrumentation of rock.

Element 2

Vocal Style

Vocal style is the way of singing — how the singer expresses emotion, technique, and expression through their voice.

Types of Vocal Styles

Belting — Powerful singing in high notes
Falsetto — Soft and high-pitched voice
Rapping — Fast rhythmic speech
Vibrato — Vibrating voice at the end of a note

💡 Example:

Regine Velasquez is known for her belting technique, while Sarah Geronimo is versatile in various vocal styles.

Element 3

Rhythm

Rhythm is the sequence of sounds and time — the beat, tempo, and meter that gives movement to the music.

Parts of Rhythm

Beat — The steady pulse of the music
Tempo — Speed of the beat (slow, moderate, fast)
Meter — Grouping of beats (4/4, 3/4, 6/8)
Syncopation — Unexpected emphasis on off-beat

💡 Example:

"We Will Rock You" by Queen has a simple 4/4 rhythm: BOOM-BOOM-CLAP! It follows the 1-2-3-4 pattern.

Element 4

Production Style

Production style is the overall technique and process in making music — from recording to mixing and mastering.

Aspects of Production

Recording — Capturing sound (studio or live)
Mixing — Blending tracks and adjusting volume
Mastering — Final polish for distribution
Effects — Reverb, delay, autotune, compression

💡 Example:

OPM pop usually has clear vocals, reverb, and electronic beats — this is the production style of modern Filipino pop.

Arts

Film Restoration

The process of restoring old films to make them clearer and higher quality.

What is Film Restoration?

Film restoration is the process of repairing and restoring old films that have deteriorated over time. It is done to preserve cultural heritage and showcase classic films in better quality.

Scanning

Digital scanning of the original film negative or print in high resolution.

Cleaning & Repair

Removing scratches, dirt, flicker, and damage from old film.

Color Correction

Adjusting colors to make the original look clear and accurate.

Audio Restoration

Cleaning the sound — removing noise and enhancing clarity.

Arts · Technology

Film & New Media

From traditional film to the digital world — how media has evolved in the modern era.

Digital Art

Film → Digital

Digital art is the creation of art using digital technology — from graphic design, animation, to VFX (visual effects) in films. It is the bridge between traditional art and modern technology.

Types of Digital Art in Film

CGI (Computer-Generated Imagery) — Effects created on computer (e.g. Avengers, Avatar)
Motion Graphics — Animated text and graphics (e.g. movie titles)
Matte Painting — Digital landscapes for backgrounds
Color Grading — Adjusting color for mood and tone

🎬 Example in Film

"Trese" (Netflix) is a Filipino animated series that used digital art and animation techniques.

🛠️ Tools for Digital Art

Adobe Photoshop After Effects Blender Procreate Maya 3D

Social Media

Film → Social Platform

Social media has become the largest platform for sharing film content. Short films, trailers, reviews, and viral videos — all on social media.

Platforms & Uses

YouTube — Full-length videos, vlogs, film reviews, tutorials
TikTok — Short-form videos, film clips, trending content
Facebook — Film groups, live streaming, community
Instagram — Reels, behind-the-scenes, film photography

💡 Impact:

Social media has given a voice to independent filmmakers — they can promote their films without a big budget.

📱 Example

Filipino content creators like "Pete's Film" and "Kimpoy Feliciano" started on social media before entering mainstream media.

Mobile Application

Film → App

Mobile applications have transformed the way we watch and create films. From streaming apps to editing tools — everything is in your hands.

Types of Film Apps

Streaming — Netflix, Viu, iWantTFC (watching)
Editing — CapCut, KineMaster, VN Video (editing)
Camera — Filmic Pro, Blackmagic Camera (shooting)
Effects — Canva, VSCO (post-processing)

💡 Impact:

Anyone can now be a filmmaker — you only need your smartphone!

📱 Example

"Untrue" (2019) by Sigrid Andrea Bernardo used smartphone camera for some scenes.

🛠️ Free Editing Apps

CapCut VN Video KineMaster InShot DaVinci Resolve

Evolution: Film → New Media

1890s — Film Era

Early films on 35mm — silent, black and white.

1950s — Television

Films entered homes through TV.

2000s — Digital Revolution

From film cameras to digital cameras — faster and more affordable.

2010s — Streaming & Social Media

Netflix, YouTube, TikTok — films are now on the internet.

Present — Mobile & AI

Smartphones and AI are the new tools for filmmaking — accessible to everyone.
